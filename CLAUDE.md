## Key Results (4 models)

### Mistral-7B (GQA 4:1) — WikiText-2 PPL
- FP16: 4.937 | TQ K4V4: 4.958 (+0.4%) | KIVI-4: 5.128 (+3.9%) | TQ K2V2: 5.789 | KIVI-2: 235 (collapse)

### Llama-3.1-8B (GQA 4:1) — WikiText-2 PPL
- FP16: 6.482 | TQ K4V4: 6.583 (+1.6%) | TQ K4V2: 6.866 | KIVI-4: 7.286 (+12.4%) | TQ K2V2: 10.347 | KIVI-2: 1244 (collapse)
- Adaptive K5.75/V2.25 (budget=4): 6.728 — uniform K4V4 beats it on GQA 4:1

### Qwen2.5-3B (GQA 8:1) — WikiText-2 PPL (headline results)
- FP16: 7.627 | TQ K6V6: 7.671 | Outlier K4V4: 7.744 | Adaptive(4.0): 8.014 | Uniform K4V4: 42.3 (collapse)
- Adaptive rescues from collapse (42→8 PPL), outlier-aware is best single method (7.74)
- Budget sweep: Adaptive 3.0→8.42, Adaptive 2.5→9.41, Uniform K3V3→1308 (collapse)

### Llama-3.1-70B (GQA 8:1)
- FP16: 3.397 (2048 tokens), 3.828 (512 tokens)
- Compressed eval OOM'd (144GB barely fits model weights, no room for KV cache overhead)

### Gemma-3-27B-it (GQA 2:1, 62 layers mixed sliding/full) — WikiText-2 PPL
- FP16: 7.468 | TQ K4V4: 7.521 (+0.7%) | TQ K4V2: 7.681 | KIVI-4: 9.136 (+22.4%) | TQ K2V2: 14.28 | KIVI-2: 439K (collapse)
- **TQ is 30x closer to FP16 than KIVI at 4-bit** — strongest TQ advantage observed
- Calibration fails (NaN on last layers — sliding attention interaction)

### Cross-Model Findings
- Keys 10-100x more sensitive than values (all models)
- GQA amplification: 8:1 ratio models (Qwen, 70B) much more sensitive to KV compression than 4:1 (Mistral, 8B)
- Outlier channels present in GQA 8:1 models (Qwen: up to 26/128), absent in GQA 4:1 (Mistral, Llama-8B)
- TQ dominates KIVI on GQA 4:1 models; KIVI more robust on GQA 8:1 at low bits
- **Lower GQA ratio → better TQ performance**: Gemma-3 (2:1) > Mistral/Llama-8B (4:1) >> Qwen (8:1)

### Hardware Benchmarks (prompt=2048, gen=32-64)

Decode throughput (tokens/sec):
| Method | Mistral-7B | Llama-8B | Qwen-3B | Gemma-3-27B |
|--------|-----------|----------|---------|-------------|
| FP16   | 37.7      | 16.0     | 24.4    | 5.5         |
| TQ K4V4| 3.8       | 2.1      | 2.4     | 1.6         |
| KIVI-4 | 7.7       | 7.7      | 7.8     | 4.4         |

- **Current implementation is Python-level, not fused CUDA kernels**
- TQ is 3-10x slower than FP16 (normalize→rotate→Lloyd-Max→unpack)
- KIVI is 2-5x slower (simpler scale/zero-point)
- Peak memory: no savings in current impl — cache.update() returns full decompressed tensors for attention
- Theoretical compression (achievable with fused kernels): TQ K4V4 = 4x, K4V2 = 5.3x, K2V2 = 8x
- **Our contribution is quality (PPL), not inference speed. Frame paper around algorithmic contributions; leave kernel fusion as future work.**
